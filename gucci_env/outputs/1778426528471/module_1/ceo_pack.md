## CEO Pack: Module 1 Outline

### Slide 1: Title Slide
*   **Module 1: Building High-Performing Teams & Talent Framework**
*   Date: [Insert Date]
*   Presented by: [Your Name/Department]
*   Objective: Align on foundational principles for talent development, team structure, and expected behaviors.

### Slide 2: Context & Problem
*   **Accelerated Growth & Evolving Market Landscape:** Our rapid expansion demands a scalable and consistent approach to talent.
*   **Inconsistent Team Performance & Development Gaps:** Observed variations in effectiveness and individual growth across the organization.
*   **Ambiguity in Career Progression:** Lack of clear expectations and pathways leads to talent frustration and retention risks.
*   **Need for a Unified Framework:** Establish a common language and set of expectations for success across all levels and functions.

### Slide 3: Principles: Autonomy vs. Group Alignment
*   **Empowering Guided Autonomy:** Foster individual and team ownership within clearly defined strategic boundaries.
*   **Strategic Cohesion:** Ensure all autonomous efforts contribute to overarching company goals and avoid siloes.
*   **Shared Responsibility & Accountability:** Emphasize that individual freedom thrives when underpinned by collective commitment to outcomes.
*   **Dynamic Balance:** Continuously re-evaluate the optimal level of autonomy versus alignment as the organization scales.

### Slide 4: Group DNA: Our Collective Identity
*   **Customer-Obsessed Innovation:** Relentless focus on delivering exceptional value and creative solutions for our users.
*   **Collaborative Excellence:** Cultivating a culture where cross-functional partnership and knowledge sharing are paramount.
*   **Data-Informed Decision Making:** Grounding strategies and actions in objective insights and continuous learning.
*   **Growth Mindset & Adaptability:** Encouraging curiosity, embracing change, and viewing challenges as opportunities for improvement.
*   **Accountability & Ownership:** Empowering individuals and teams to take full responsibility for their commitments and results.

### Slide 5: Competency Model Overview
*   **Purpose:** A clear, consistent framework for defining, assessing, and developing critical skills and behaviors across the organization.
*   **Structure:** Organized around core competencies (e.g., Strategic Thinking, Execution, Communication, Leadership).
*   **Benefits:** Enhances consistency in hiring, performance management, career development, and succession planning.
*   **Foundation for Future Modules:** This core model will be expanded with functional-specific competencies in subsequent phases.

### Slide 6: Level Behaviors: Defining Excellence at Each Stage
*   **Translating Competencies to Levels:** How specific behaviors and expectations evolve from individual contributor to senior leadership.
*   **Clear Expectations:** Provides transparent criteria for what is expected at each professional growth stage.
*   **Enabling Career Progression:** Offers a practical roadmap for individuals to understand how to develop and advance.
*   **Objective Assessment:** Facilitates more consistent, fair, and objective performance reviews and promotion decisions.

### Slide 7: Use Cases & Impact
*   **Enhanced Talent Acquisition:** Standardized criteria for evaluating candidates and accelerating new hire ramp-up.
*   **Targeted Development & Training:** Identifying specific skill gaps and tailoring programs for individuals and teams.
*   **Effective Performance Management:** Providing objective feedback, facilitating constructive conversations, and setting clear goals.
*   **Strategic Workforce Planning:** Better understanding of organizational capabilities to optimize team formation and resource allocation.

### Slide 8: Rollout Preview (Module 1 Focus)
*   **Phase 1: Leadership & Manager Training (Q3):** Workshops to ensure deep understanding and practical application of the model.
*   **Phase 2: HRIS Integration & Tooling (Q4):** Embedding competencies into our HR systems for seamless use in performance cycles.
*   **Phase 3: Pilot Programs (Q4/Q1 next year):** Launching with select teams to gather feedback and refine implementation.
*   **Ongoing Communication & Resources:** Developing internal materials, FAQs, and support channels to ensure broad adoption.

### Slide 9: Asks & Key Decisions
*   **Alignment & Endorsement:** Secure leadership's full support for the "Guided Autonomy" principle and the outlined Group DNA.
*   **Approval of Core Competency Framework:** Confirm buy-in for the overarching structure and initial set of core competencies.
*   **Resource Commitment:** Ensure adequate budget and personnel allocation for successful Module 1 implementation.
*   **Executive Sponsorship:** Identify key champions to actively promote and drive the adoption of the new talent framework.

### Slide 10: Next Steps & Q&A
*   **Next Steps:** Incorporate feedback, finalize Module 1 materials, initiate manager training development.
*   **Upcoming Modules:** Focus on functional competencies, advanced leadership development, and reward structures.
*   **Open Discussion:** Address any questions or concerns from the leadership team.
*   **Thank You:** Express gratitude for their time and strategic partnership in building a high-performing organization.
